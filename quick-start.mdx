title: "Quick Start: Make Your First Call in Minutes"
description: "Sign up for Vobiz, buy a phone number, and configure your first SIP trunk or XML application. Follow this step-by-step guide to make your first call."
---

This guide walks you through the essential setup steps to get your Vobiz account ready to make and receive calls. By the end, you will have a phone number, a working SIP trunk or XML application, and a clear picture of where to go next.

<Steps>

<Step title="Sign up">

Create your Vobiz account at the [registration page](https://console.vobiz.ai/app/register). Registration takes under two minutes — you only need an email address and a password.

Once your account is created, Vobiz credits your account with ₹25 to explore the platform before adding funds.

</Step>

<Step title="Review the dashboard">

After signing up you land on the dashboard. From here you can see your account balance, navigate to all sections of the console, and monitor recent activity.

<Note>
  Your **Auth ID** and **Auth Token** are visible in Account Settings. You will need both values to authenticate every API request.
</Note>

</Step>

<Step title="Buy a phone number">

Navigate to the **DID** section of the console. Filter numbers by region to find a number that matches your target geography, then purchase it.

The number is immediately available for inbound and outbound routing once provisioned.

</Step>

<Step title="Set up SIP trunks">

SIP trunks connect your infrastructure to the Vobiz carrier network. Configure outbound and inbound trunks separately.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Outbound trunk">
    1. Go to **SIP Trunks → Outbound** in the console.
    2. Create a new outbound trunk and give it a name.
    3. Create credentials (username and password) for the trunk. These authenticate your SIP INVITE requests to the Vobiz network.

    Your outbound trunk is now ready to send calls from your SIP infrastructure.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Inbound trunk">
    1. Go to **SIP Trunks → Inbound** in the console.
    2. Link your purchased phone number to the inbound trunk. Calls to that DID are forwarded to the trunk.
    3. Optionally, configure a **SIP Origination URI** to route inbound calls directly to your own SIP server or hosted platform (LiveKit, VAPI, Retell AI, etc.).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  If you are connecting to VAPI, Retell AI, LiveKit, or ElevenLabs, point your inbound trunk's SIP URI at the platform's SIP endpoint. See the [Integrations](/integrations) section for platform-specific instructions.
</Tip>

</Step>

<Step title="Connect platform integrations">

Vobiz integrates with a wide range of AI voice and telephony platforms. Configure the integration that matches your stack:

- [VAPI](/integrations/vapi-dashboard) — Managed AI voice with BYO SIP trunk
- [Retell AI](/integrations/retellai-dashboard) — Elastic SIP or API registration
- [ElevenLabs](/integrations/elevenlabs-dashboard) — Native SIP for conversational AI
- [LiveKit](/integrations/livekit) — SIP Service → WebRTC room
- [Pipecat](/integrations/pipecat) — WebSocket audio streaming
- [View all integrations →](/integrations)

</Step>

<Step title="Create an XML application">

If you want to control call flow programmatically without a managed platform, create a **Voice XML application** in the **Voice** section of the console.

Configure the following URLs on your application:

| URL field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Answer URL | Vobiz fetches this URL when a call is answered. Return Voice XML to control the call. |
| Fallback URL | Used if the Answer URL is unreachable. |
| Hangup URL | Called when the call ends. |
| Status URL | Receives status callback events (Ring, StartApp, Hangup). |

After creating the application, assign your purchased phone number to it. Inbound calls to that number will execute your XML application logic.

<Tip>
  Use the [XML Builder](https://www.docs.vobiz.ai/xml-builder) to generate Voice XML quickly without writing it by hand.
</Tip>

</Step>

<Step title="View transactions">

Go to the **Transactions** section to see a full history of charges and credits on your account. Monitor balance consumption as you make calls during testing.

</Step>

<Step title="View voice call logs">

The **Voice Call Logs** section shows records for all calls handled through XML Applications and the Voice REST API. Use this view to confirm calls are processing correctly and to inspect call metadata at a high level.

</Step>

<Step title="Manage recordings">

If your Voice XML application includes a `<Record>` directive, your recordings appear in the **Recordings** section. You can listen to, download, or delete recordings from this view.

</Step>

<Step title="View SIP call logs">

For calls made through SIP Trunks or SIP Endpoints, check **SIP Call Logs**. This section provides low-level SIP signaling details — response codes, trunk ID, hangup cause — essential for diagnosing connection issues.

<Note>
  If you see SIP 480 or 486 response codes, see [SIP codes 480 and 486](/faq#why-am-i-receiving-sip-response-codes-480-or-486) in the FAQ for an explanation.
</Note>

</Step>

</Steps>
