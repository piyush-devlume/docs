Use the balance transfer endpoint to fund a customer's wallet by moving credits from your partner master account. This is the primary mechanism for reselling voice minutes: you hold the balance, you allocate it to customers, and they spend it on calls.

Transfers are **atomic** (they complete fully or not at all), **instant** (the customer's wallet is credited immediately), and **permanent** (they cannot be reversed via the API).

## Endpoint

**`POST https://api.vobiz.ai/api/v1/partner/accounts/{customer_auth_id}/transfer-balance`**

## Path parameters

<ParamField path="customer_auth_id" type="string" required>
  The `auth_id` of the customer account to fund. Retrieve this from the [List customer accounts](/partner/api/customers) endpoint.
</ParamField>

## Request body

<ParamField body="amount" type="number" required>
  Amount to transfer as a decimal (e.g. `500.00`). Must be a positive number. Your master balance must be greater than or equal to this amount at the time of the request.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="currency" type="string" required>
  Currency code. Must match your partner account currency (e.g. `INR`).
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="description" type="string">
  Optional note for your bookkeeping records. Appears in both your partner debit ledger and the customer's credit ledger.
</ParamField>

## Response

Returns HTTP `200 OK` on success.

<ResponseField name="transaction_id" type="string">
  Unique identifier for this transfer. Store this for deduplication and dispute resolution.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="from_account" type="string">
  Your partner ID (the account debited).
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="to_account" type="string">
  The customer `auth_id` (the account credited).
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="amount" type="number">
  Amount transferred.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="currency" type="string">
  Currency of the transfer.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="description" type="string">
  The description you provided, if any.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="status" type="string">
  Always `"completed"` on a successful response.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="partner_balance_after" type="number">
  Your master wallet balance after the debit.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="customer_balance_after" type="number">
  The customer's wallet balance after the credit.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="timestamp" type="string">
  ISO 8601 timestamp of the transfer.
</ResponseField>

## Code examples

<CodeGroup>

```bash cURL
curl -X POST \
  "https://api.vobiz.ai/api/v1/partner/accounts/{customer_auth_id}/transfer-balance" \
  -H "X-Auth-ID: {your_partner_id}" \
  -H "X-Auth-Token: {your_auth_token}" \
  -H "Accept: application/json" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "amount": 500.00,
    "currency": "INR",
    "description": "April recharge — Credresolve"
  }'
```

```python Python
import requests

BASE = "https://api.vobiz.ai/api/v1/partner"
HEADERS = {
    "X-Auth-ID": "{your_partner_id}",
    "X-Auth-Token": "{your_auth_token}",
    "Accept": "application/json",
    "Content-Type": "application/json",
}

# Check your own balance first
profile = requests.get(f"{BASE}/me", headers=HEADERS).json()
if profile["balance"] < 500.00:
    raise Exception(f"Insufficient partner balance: {profile['balance']}")

# Transfer
response = requests.post(
    f"{BASE}/accounts/{{customer_auth_id}}/transfer-balance",
    headers=HEADERS,
    json={
        "amount": 500.00,
        "currency": "INR",
        "description": "April recharge — Credresolve",
    },
)
print(response.json())
```

## Pre-transfer checklist

<Warning>
  Transfers are permanent. There is no API endpoint to reverse a completed transfer. If you transfer the wrong amount or to the wrong customer, contact Vobiz support. Always verify before submitting.
</Warning>

Before calling this endpoint:

1. Verify the `customer_auth_id` by calling `GET /accounts/{id}` and confirming the account name matches your intent.
2. Check your own balance via `GET /me` to confirm it exceeds the transfer amount.
3. Confirm the `currency` matches your partner account currency.
4. Add a descriptive `description` for easier ledger reconciliation.
5. In production, store the returned `transaction_id` to detect and prevent duplicate transfers.
